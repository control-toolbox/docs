    Simple exponential: energy minimisation

    The energy minimisation simple exponential problem consists in minimising

    \[ \frac{1}{2}\int_{0}^{1} u^2(t) \, \mathrm{d}t\]

    subject to the constraints

    \[ \dot x(t) = - x(t) + u(t), u(t) \in \mathbb{R}\]

    and the limit conditions

    \[ x(0) = -1, \quad x(1) = 0.\]

    You can access the problem in the CTProblems package:

    using CTProblems
prob = Problem(:exponential, :energy)
    title           = simple exponential - energy min
model    (Type) = CTBase.OptimalControlModel{CTBase.Autonomous, CTBase.Fixed}
solution (Type) = CTBase.OptimalControlSolution

    Then, the model is given by

    prob.model
    
t ∈ [t0, tf], time
x ∈ R, state
u ∈ R, control
x(t0) == x0, initial_con
x(tf) == xf, final_con
ẋ(t) == -(x(t)) + u(t)
∫(0.5 * u(t) ^ 2) → min

    You can plot the solution.

    plot(prob.solution, size=(700, 400))
